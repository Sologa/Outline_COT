Flat concept inventory:
- \textbf{Puzzle Categories}
- \textbf{Rule-less puzzles:} rely more on flexible thinking, real-world knowledge and inferential reasoning
- \textbf{Commonsense reasoning puzzles:} require understanding real-world situations and making inferences based on implicit knowledge
- \textbf{Programming puzzles:} involve analyzing or modifying code snippets to achieve a specific goal
- \textbf{Riddles:} use wordplay and metaphors to conceal the answers, requiring abstract connections and lateral thinking
- \textbf{Rule-based puzzles:} provide explicit victory conditions, legal move sets or state transition rules that the model must follow to solve the puzzle
- \textbf{Stochastic games:} incorporate randomness or hidden information, resulting in different outcomes
- \textbf{Deterministic games:} provide all the information needed to produce an outcome from a given starting state and set of actions
