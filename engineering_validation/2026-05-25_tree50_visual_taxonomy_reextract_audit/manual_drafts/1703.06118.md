## Evidence

- Figure 2 visually contains the full two-branch tree.
- `main.tex` around the Figure 2 include/caption says the figure depicts the
  taxonomy.
- Later taxonomy prose confirms the main branches: domain / Data Science,
  problem / scope, and data / methods.
- Figure 4 (`methods.pdf`) is included as a refinement of the Figure 2 `Method`
  branch because `main.tex` says methods were divided into five groups according
  to that figure.
- Figure 3 (`flightdelayprediction.pdf`) is included under `Data -> Dimensions`
  as an auxiliary data-model facet because `main.tex` says the commonly used
  attributes were organized into seven classes depicted in the data model.

## Original V2 Issue

- Existing v2 stopped at a compact 9-node tree.
- It missed `Data sources`, `Dimensions`, all five `Method` leaves, all
  `Scope` leaves, and the split between `New (root) Delay` and `Cancellation`.
- The first round manual draft also only captured Figure 2; this revision adds
  Figure 4 as a method refinement and Figure 3 as an auxiliary dimensions facet.

## Uncertainties

- Figure label is `Data science`; prose uses `Data Science`.
- Figure uses `Method`; prose sometimes says `methods`.
- `New (root) Delay` is the visible figure label; prose also discusses
  `root delay and cancellation` as a combined category.
- Figure 3 is a `Data model`, not an independent primary taxonomy tree. It is
  included under `Dimensions`, but should be counted as an auxiliary facet rather
  than a separate strict taxonomy tree.
